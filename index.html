This project implements a 1D convolutional neural network in PyTorch, trained to classify ECG signals from the MIT-BIH dataset. The dataset consisted of over eighty-thousand samples assigned to 5 different categories of arrhythmia. It would be fair to say that the dataset was pretty considerably unbalanced, however, up sampling didn't seem to effect performance at all. The model architecture I used was two sets of convolutional, batch norm, max pooling, with dense layers used for classification. I produced learning curves to visualise both the change over time in training loss and accuracy, as well as validation loss and accuracy. I also produced a confusion matrix to display how well this model makes predictions. Overall the model fit the data very well, achieving 97.5% validation accuracy after only one epoch. Click the graph to load a random signal from a fully balanced, hundred sample subset of the data I used for validation.
